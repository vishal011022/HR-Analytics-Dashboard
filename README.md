# HR Analytics Dashboard
## 🔍 Overview
This HR Analytics Dashboard is built to help HR teams and business leaders understand employee attrition trends across departments, age groups, job roles, education fields, and more. It enables data-driven decision-making to reduce turnover and improve employee retention strategies.

## 📌 Key Metrics
### Matrics         -     Values
👥 Total Employees  -     1,470

🚪 Attrition Count  -     237

📉 Attrition Rate   -     16.1%

✅ Active Employees -     1,233

🎂 Average Age     -      37

## 📊 Dashboard Sections
### 1. 🏢 Attrition by Department
Compares attrition rates across Sales, HR, and R&D departments, segmented by gender.

**HR** has the highest attrition rate at **44.0%**

**R**&D follows at **26.8%**

**Sales** at **41.1%**

## 2. 👶 Attrition by Age Group
Visualizes which age bands experience the most attrition.

The **25–34** age group has the highest attrition count **(112)**

**Under 25 and 35–44** are also significant segments

## 3. ⚧ Attrition Count by Gender

**Male**: 150 attritions

**Female**: 87 attritions

## 4. 💍 Attrition by Marital Status & Gender

**Single** employees have the highest attrition (120)

Followed by **Married** (84) and **Divorced** (33)

## 5. 🎓 Attrition by Education Field & Gender

**Life Sciences** leads with 89 total attritions (51 male, 38 female)

**Medical** and **Marketing** fields also show notable numbers

## 💡 Insights & Takeaways

**Sales Representatives** face nearly **40% attrition** — the highest of any role

**Young employees (25–34)** are at the highest risk of leaving

**Single employees** are significantly more likely to leave than married ones

**Life Sciences & Medical** graduates make up the majority of attrition cases

The **HR department** has a disproportionately high attrition rate (44%)
